Ti-ker-ba-bin

Description

Tikerbabin is a North African dish, popular in Kabyle regions, its preparation varies according to regions but generally it consists of semolina balls infused in coriander and/or mint and soaked in a red tomato sauce, vegetables, and chicken and/or meat, some versions use lentil soup instead.

Adapted from Mes Inspirations Culinaires's recipe

Ingredients (4 servings)

For the sauce:

  • Onions
  • Vegetables of your choice: carrots, zucchini...
  • a big handful of chickpeas
  • a handful of lentils
  • a handful of black eyed peas
  • 1 tbsp of red pepper

For the semolina balls:

  • 7 measures of thick semolina
  • 1 measure of oil (50/50 of cooking oil and olive oil)
  • 1 onion
  • 1-2 bell peppers
  • 1 tomato
  • 1/2 bunch of fresh mint
  • 1/2 bunch of fresh coriander
  • 1 tsp of red pepper
  • 1 tbsp of dried mint

Steps

  1. Cut the vegetables into small pieces.
  2. In a pot, brown the chopped onions and the spices. Add the pieces of chicken or meat. Brown for about 10 minutes.
  3. Add the water and immerse the chickpeas and the white beans.
  4. Pour in the water (about 2 liters) and bring to a boil over medium heat, covered.
  5. In the meantime, prepare the semolina balls: Mix together the pepper, onion, mint, coriander and tomato.
  6. Mix the semolina as well as the oil (mixture of olive and sunflower oil), salt, pepper, red pepper, dry mint.
  7. Pour the minced vegetables and mix (without kneading) adding water until you can make balls that hold together.
  8. Dip the balls gently into the pot with a handful of lentils and the remaining vegetables.
  9. Let cook for about 35-40 minutes.
  10. Add a tablespoon of tomato sauce and mint.
  11. Enjoy while still hot!
